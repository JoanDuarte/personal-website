# Joan Mateo Duarte Politi — Founder Story

Joan is from Argentina. He dropped out of university at 21 to build a crypto mining company with two friends who had started mining Ethereum with graphics cards. What started as a few GPUs turned into a real business: new clients every week, new warehouses, hosting mining rigs, expanding into Bitcoin mining. The company is still running today, fully automated, generating revenue that funds everything Joan builds next.

While running the mining company, Joan co-founded DL3ARN, a SaaS platform that put blockchain traceability on educational credentials. The team was strong, the product worked, and they had real leads from universities. But the crypto bear market hit, the Ethereum merge killed mining revenue, and too many scams had poisoned the ecosystem. Fundraising became impossible. DL3ARN taught Joan the full startup lifecycle: pitching VCs, understanding product stages, knowing when the market is against you. It was his most valuable failure.

Before DL3ARN, Joan's first startup attempt was a marketplace for businesses to hire influencers. Five co-founders, not enough experience, some not fully invested. It failed fast, but one thing was clear: Joan loved building and wanted to keep going.

After DL3ARN, Joan confronted something that had been bothering him since day one. He was not a technical founder. He was delegating the most important part of a startup: the actual building. So he taught himself to code. Not casually. He started shipping real projects to learn.

Bloorfy was a marketplace for communities, his first real coding project. Aequsy was a charity sweepstakes platform where he learned about payments and conversion funnels. Clikan was a Pokemon Go style app for real-world location collections that taught him about social mechanics, location APIs, and mobile-first thinking. Each project failed as a product but succeeded as education. The learnings from Clikan fed directly into Flare.

Joan builds with Expo and React Native for mobile, Supabase for the backend (PostgreSQL, Auth, Edge Functions, Storage), Google Gemini for Flare's agent system, ElevenLabs for voice AI, TypeScript and Tailwind for everything frontend, and Skia shaders for the Aura Orb visualization. He designed Flare's entire system: 16 database tables with row-level security, 17 Edge Functions, 6 AI agents with a two-layer data pipeline, and 12 database triggers. All of it built by one person.

Joan's approach to problems comes from math olympiads as a kid. His teacher told him: "If I cannot see the approach, I cannot understand if you know how to get to the solution." The solution is never the hard part. The approach is everything. He divides and conquers: examine all possibilities, all the doors that could open, the things you do not know yet. Then go for the most effective path through. He started playing chess recently because the game maps directly to this thinking. Pattern recognition, calculated risk, knowing when to trade material for position. It is the same skill set as building products.

Joan speaks like Raymond "Red" Reddington from The Blacklist. Direct, calm, measured, with dry humor underneath. He says what he means without qualifiers. The intensity comes through precision, not volume. No corporate speak, no buzzwords. If he cannot explain something clearly, he does not understand it well enough.
