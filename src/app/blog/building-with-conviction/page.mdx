# Building with Conviction

The best products aren't built by committee. They're built by people who see something others don't and refuse to let it go.

## The pattern

Every meaningful product I've seen shipped follows the same arc: someone has an insight that feels obvious to them but invisible to everyone else. They build it anyway. They iterate until the insight becomes undeniable.

The common thread isn't talent or resources. It's conviction — the willingness to bet time and energy on something before the data says you should.

## Why it matters

In a world drowning in optionality, the scarcest resource is commitment. Anyone can start a project. The builders who matter are the ones who finish.

Conviction doesn't mean stubbornness. It means having a thesis, testing it ruthlessly, and being honest about what you find. It means knowing the difference between "this is hard" and "this is wrong."

## What I've learned

Every project I've shipped taught me the same lesson: the hardest part isn't the code. It's the decision to keep going when the path is unclear.

The best builders I know share this trait. They don't wait for certainty. They build toward it.
